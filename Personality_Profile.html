Personality Profile

Here are the results from 3 different personality tests, and my interpretation of them and how they may affect me going into a team situation.

1) Myers-Briggs Personality Profile:

What do the results of these tests mean for you?

This test result provides an eerily accurate portrayal of many of my personality traits. It explains that I have a very empathetic nature, going out of my way to help others when possible. It also showcases that I have a strong desire to lead that may sometimes come across the wrong way.

How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team and forming a team?

I do like to take on leadership roles as explained in the results, however if someone else wants to take that role I am more than happy to step aside and let them take charge as I like to see others succeed. I just need to remember to let everyone have a voice and ensure I don’t come across condescending or judgemental in my explanations. As long as I am aware of these factors, I am able to keep myself in check and ensure that I am always a team player, and cheerleader for everyone else succeeding.

2) Learning Styles Profile:

What do the results of these tests mean for you?

This test relates more to how of learning and accurately describes me as someone that learns through doing rather than through theory. I am very hands on and always pick something up faster when able to implement a skill rather than just writing/reading about it.

How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team and forming a team?

As for team interaction, when someone is explaining a concept that is new to me, if I am struggling to grasp the information, I just need to be transparent and let them know that I may need to go away, apply the knowledge to some form of a practical example, so I can then understand what they’re trying to explain. Ensuring I’m in a team with people that have a similar learning style would be advantageous as you can learn in a group activity rather than always needing to step away to learn.

3) The Big 5 Personality Test:

What do the results of these tests mean for you?

These test results delve quite deep into a personality profile and hit some very personal notes. Some points are rather accurate, such as that I have a very open and positive outlook at most times, or that it takes time to open up to people. While other points, such as the Anxiety, Anger, and Depression results, miss the mark and paint traits that I have as being much more severe than they truly are.

How do you think these results may influence your behaviour in a team and forming a team?

As I can take a little while to warm up to people, I may seem reserved at first so I must ensure I try and be open from the beginning. That said, if I see other people are uncomfortable, I always try to include everyone. Due to this it doesn’t really change how I am in a team as I am adaptable to the situation. Basically, if I must be open and social from the get-go to ease others peace of mind I can.

Note: As these tests are all made in a generic way, their results should be taken with a grain of salt. While there are accuracies in some answers not every ‘personality test’ is going to cover all of someone traits and behaviours, much in the same way that someone’s Zodiac sign will show similarity to them but not always fully encompass who they are.
